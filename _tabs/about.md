## The Roman Catholic Diocese of Kalookan
The Diocese of Kalookan was created by **Pope John Paul II** on June 28, 2003, through the apostolic letter *Quoniam Quælibet*. It was formally established on August 22, 2003, following the division of the Archdiocese of Manila into several new dioceses.

### Territorial Jurisdiction
The diocese serves the southern part of **Caloocan City**, and the cities of **Malabon** and **Navotas**. It comprises approximately 31 parishes and several mission stations, serving a vibrant Catholic community in Northern Metro Manila.

  <div style="flex: 2; min-width: 300px;">
    <p>The symbols represent the three jurisdictions that comprise the Diocese:</p>
    <ul>
      <li><strong>The Monument (Center):</strong> Represents Caloocan City, from which the name of the Diocese was derived.</li>
      <li><strong>The Fishes (Right):</strong> Represent the City of Malabon.</li>
      <li><strong>The Banca (Left):</strong> Represents the Municipality of Navotas.</li>
      <li><strong>The Cross & Dove:</strong> The Cross signifies the centrality of Jesus Christ, while the Dove represents the Holy Spirit sanctifying the People of God.</li>
    </ul>
    <p><strong>Symbolism of Colors:</strong></p>
    <ul>
        <li><span style="color: #c0392b; font-weight: bold;">Red (Lower Half):</span> Symbolizes the valor of martyrdom and the sanguine ardor, fortitude, and love of the Diocese.</li>
        <li><span style="color: #2980b9; font-weight: bold;">Blue (Upper Half):</span> Denotes the Marian character of the diocesan ministry.</li>
    </ul>
    <blockquote style="border-left: 5px solid #2980b9; padding-left: 15px; font-style: italic;">
        <strong>Motto:</strong> “DUC IN ALTUM” (Lk. 5:4) – “Put out into the deep”
    </blockquote>

</div>
